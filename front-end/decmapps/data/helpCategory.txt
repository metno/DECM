SPI and SPEI values are divided into categories which describe
different levels of wetness or dryness with respect to the median (scaled to zero). 
<br>
<br>
The used categories are: <br> <br>
  1. Extremely wet (EW) = values 2 standard deviations <i>above</i> the median <br> <br>
  2. very wet or wetter (EW+VW) = values 1.5 standard deviations or more <i>above</i> the median <br> <br>
  3. moderately wet or wetter (EW+VW+MW) = values 1.0 standard deviations or more <i>above</i> the median <br> <br>
  4. moderately dry or drier (MD+SD+ED) = values 1.0 standard deviations or more <i>below</i> the median <br> <br>
  5. severely dry or drier (SD+ED) = values 1.5 standard deviations or more <i>below</i> the median <br> <br>
  6. extremely dry (EW+ VW) = values 2 standard deviations or more <i>below</i> the median <br>
